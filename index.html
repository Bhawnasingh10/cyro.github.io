What we do.

2D Class Averaging

2D averaging is a computational method used to improve the clarity and resolution of a molecule's structure. It involves aligning and averaging multiple 2D images obtained through electron microscopy or imaging techniques.

3d structure reconstruction

3D structure reconstruction is a technique used to determine the three-dimensional arrangement of molecules, such as proteins or nucleic acids.

Feasibiility Assesment

Feasibility assessment in structural biology involves evaluating the practicality and likelihood of obtaining structural information for a biomolecule.

Negative Stain Screening

Negative stain screening is a technique in structural biology that uses electron microscopy to quickly assess the shape and structure of macromolecules.

Single Particle Analysis

Single particle analysis is a powerful technique in structural biology that utilizes electron microscopy to determine the three-dimensional structure of macromolecules.
